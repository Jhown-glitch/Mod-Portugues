1968:\nThe Soviet Union, under the cover of night, invaded Czechoslovakia with 250,000 soldiers and 2,000 tanks to crush the reformist government there. Seeing a complete lack of attention on the part of America, who was at the time fighting Soviet troops in Vietnam, the emboldened Brezhnev regime decided that it was time to increase the sphere of communist influence in Europe. Their target? West Germany. Warsaw Pact navies still rely on relatively outdated surface warships, but their submarines equipped with cruise missiles still pose a threat to NATO supply convoys.
